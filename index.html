ISL Core Features

Indian Sign Language (ISL) offers a variety of features and functionalities that cater to the needs of its users.

Some of the key features provided by ISL include:

Visual Communication:

ISL primarily relies on gestures, hand movements, facial expressions, and body language to convey meaning, making it a visual and expressive form of communication.

Technological Integration:

ISL is increasingly being integrated into digital platforms and technologies, such as video conferencing, educational videos, and mobile applications, to enhance accessibility and communication.

The Indian Sign Language (ISL) system provides various services to support the deaf and hard of hearing community. These services include interpretation for communication in different settings, educational programs, and online learning resources. ISL also ensures accessibility through captioning, public information translation, and job placement assistance. In healthcare, ISL interpreters help patients communicate with doctors, and health information is provided in ISL. Legal support includes court interpreters and legal advocacy. Community services offer counseling, support groups, and accessible events. Technology services develop communication tools and accessibility features for digital platforms. Advocacy efforts raise public awareness and promote the rights of deaf individuals, and emergency services ensure deaf people receive crucial information during crises.
